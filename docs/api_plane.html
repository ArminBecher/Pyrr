Plane¶

Provide functions for the creation and manipulation of Planes.

pyrr.plane.create_from_points(vector1, vector2, vector3)[source]¶

Create a plane from 3 co-planar vectors.

The vectors must all lie on the same plane or an exception will be thrown.

The vectors must not all be in a single line or the plane is undefined.

The order the vertices are passed in will determine the normal of the plane.

@param vector1: a vector that lies on the desired plane. @param vector2: a vector that lies on the desired plane. @param vector3: a vector that lies on the desired plane. @raise ValueError: raised if the vectors are not co-planar. @raise ValueError: raised if the vectors are in a single line

pyrr.plane.create_from_position(position, normal, up)[source]¶

Creates a plane at position with the normal being above the plane and up being the rotation of the plane. This is required as a plane must be defined by 3 vectors otherwise rotation is undefined.

@param position: The position of the plane. @param normal: The normal will be normalised during construction. @param up: Must be co-planar. The up vector will be normalised during construction. The up vector must lie on the plane itself. Without it, the plane is undefined. @raise ValueError: Raised if the up vector is not co-planar.

pyrr.plane.create_identity()[source]¶

Creates a plane at the origin, with a normal of 0,0,1 and up of 0,1,0.

This is a plane that lies at the origin on the X,Y plane and faces +Z.

pyrr.plane.flip_normal(plane)[source]¶

Flips the normal of the plane in place.

@return Returns the plane for convenience.
